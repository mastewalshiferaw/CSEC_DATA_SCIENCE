# Strategic Analysis of the Netflix Content Ecosystem

## Insight 1: Cohort Dynamics
Analyzing the `year_added` cohort reveals that **2019** was the inflection point where the volume of Netflix Originals began to cannibalize the growth rate of licensed content. This marks a strategic shift from a **distribution-focused model** to an **IP ownership model**, where Netflix increasingly prioritizes owning content rather than licensing it.

## Insight 2: Distribution Strategy
A crosstab analysis shows that **International Movies** are disproportionately rated **TV-MA** compared to domestic US titles. This indicates a localization strategy that emphasizes **high-impact, mature content** to effectively penetrate diverse global markets and cater to regional audience preferences.

## Insight 3: Correlation Anomaly
There is a near-zero correlation between `release_year` and `duration`. This contradicts the common assumption that newer content tends to be shorter. Instead, it suggests that Netflix’s programming strategy is **agnostic to runtime**, focusing more on **binge-ability** than traditional length constraints.

## Insight 4: The 95th Percentile Rule
Quantile analysis shows that the **95th percentile for `release_year` is 2021**, meaning only about 5% of the library consists of older content. This supports Netflix’s **"Shelf-Life" strategy**, where content is treated as a **perishable asset** rather than a long-term archive.

## Insight 5: Market Specialization
Ratio analysis highlights distinct market archetypes:
- **US/India Model** → Movie-heavy content mix  
- **Korea/Japan Model** → Series-heavy content mix  

This demonstrates that Netflix does not apply a one-size-fits-all strategy, but instead **customizes its content portfolio** based on regional consumption behaviors.
